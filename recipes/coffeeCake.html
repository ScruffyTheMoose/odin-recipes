Coffee Cake

Description

My inspiration for this classic crumb coffee cake is the picture-perfect slices you find behind the glass at Starbucks—but bigger, better, and with more crumb!

The cake batter includes sour cream, which not only adds richness but also helps maintain a tender crumb. There’s a cinnamon stripe through the center of the cake and a blanket of crumble over the top.

To ensure that distinctive stripe, we layer about half of the batter into the bottom of the pan, sprinkle the cinnamon layer over the top, then the remaining batter, and finally, a thick cover of crumble topping.

Details

  • Prep Time: 25 minutes
  • Cook Time: 55 minutes
  • Total Time: 80 minutes
  • Servings: 15 to 18

Ingredients

    For the topping

  • 1 cup (180g) brown sugar, packed
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of Kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup (113g) softened unsalted butter

    • For the cinnamon layer

  • 1/4 cup (50g) sugar
  • 1 tablesppon all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

    • For the cake batter

  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/4 cups (450g) sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 cup (226g) softened unsalted butter
  • 1 cup (230g) sour cream
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and butter or spray a 9 x 13 inch pan.
  2. Make the streusel layer by combining the flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt in a small bowl. Use a fork to mash the butter into the mixture until it is brown and crumbly.
  3. Make the cinnamon layer by combining the sugar, flour, and cinnmon in a seperate bowl.

  4. Begin making the batter by mixing the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and kosher salt in a large bowl. If you have a stand mixer, stir on low with a paddle attachment.

    Add the softened butter and beat on medium-low speed for 2 to 3 minutes or until the mixture resembled wet sand.

  5. Combine the wet incredients and finish the batter by wisking the remains together. This includes the sour cream, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  6. Prepare the cake by spreading half of the batter into the pan. Then, sprinle on the cinnamon layer before adding the remaining half of the batter on top. Finally, top it all off with your crumbles.
  7. Bake the cake for 55 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean. Cool to room temperature before serving.
